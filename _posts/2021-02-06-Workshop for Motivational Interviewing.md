I took the workshop for Motivational Interviewing by [Prof. Sung-Man Shin](http://pro.handong.edu/%EB%AA%BD%EB%B8%94%EB%9E%91/). <br/>
The lecture addressed 'motivation' based on the self-determination theory by Richard Ryan & Edward Deci. <br/>
It was interesting that the theory defines an intrinsic motivation as a combination of autonomy, belonging, competence, and a sense of goal. <br/>
After learning these theoretical backgrounds, we could practice motivation interviewing in the practice session.<br/>
I tried the role of a counselor, and I practiced the OARS techniques (Open questions, Affirming, Reflection, and Summarizing). <br/>
I look forward to applying what I've learned in this workshop at the 1:1 counseling session that I'm going to do in the On-campus counseling center :)
